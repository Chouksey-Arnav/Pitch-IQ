📹 Filming Tips
✓
Camera position
Side-on at waist height, 5–10m away. The camera should see your full body with some space above and below.
✓
Lighting
Overcast outdoor is ideal. Avoid harsh backlight or deep indoor shadows — these reduce AI detection accuracy significantly.
✓
Clothing
Fitted clothing that contrasts your background. Blue shirt against green outfield = easy. Loose whites against white sightscreen = hard.
✓
Frame selection
Batting: pause at bat-ball contact. Bowling: pause as the front foot lands in the crease. Use ← → arrow keys for single-frame precision.
⚡ Tool Guide
🦴
Live Track
AI skeleton overlays on the video as it plays. Joints turn green, amber, or red based on your angles in real time.
📸
Kinogram
Captures N skeleton poses as video plays and composites them into a single image showing your full motion arc. Beloved by professional coaches worldwide.
👻
Skeleton Only Mode
Hides the video background — shows pure skeleton on a dark surface. Removes visual noise so you can focus purely on body position.
📈
Live Angle Graph
Shows your elbow and shoulder angles plotted over time as the video plays. Watch exactly which frame your technique changes.
✏️
Drawing Tools
Draw lines and annotations directly on the video to mark reference points, ideal positions, or share with your coach.
