Questions

Everything you need to know.

Your video is analyzed entirely within your browser — it's processed by your own device's CPU and GPU, never transmitted over the internet. You can verify this yourself: load the page, then disconnect your Wi-Fi entirely. The analysis still runs. Nothing about your footage ever reaches our servers. We don't have servers for video analysis — by design.
Side-on angle works best — camera at waist height, 5–10 metres away. Most smartphone cameras are perfectly sufficient. Good lighting is the biggest factor: natural outdoor light (even overcast) is ideal. Wear fitted clothing that contrasts with the background — a blue shirt against a green outfield is easy to detect; loose whites against a white sightscreen is harder. Frame yourself fully in the shot with some space above your head and below your feet.
When you hit play, our AI processes each frame of your video as it plays and draws a skeleton overlay on top of the footage in real time. You can watch your joints being tracked at 25–30 frames per second. When you see a moment you want deeply analyzed — bat-ball contact, front foot landing — simply pause the video and click Analyze. The AI then runs a detailed biomechanical assessment on that specific frame.
The real-time skeleton tracking is always unlimited — you can watch your skeleton overlay play through any video as many times as you want. "Analyses" refers to the detailed deep-dive reports: joint angle measurements, coaching feedback, and drill recommendations. Free users can generate 3 of these per day. The counter resets automatically at midnight every night. Premium removes this limit entirely.
The AI tracking technology we use is the same that powers professional sports analysis platforms worldwide. On good-quality side-on footage in reasonable lighting, joint angle measurements are highly reliable. That said, PitchIQ is a supplement to coaching, not a replacement for it. The ideal workflow: use PitchIQ to identify issues between coaching sessions, bring your report to your next session, and let your coach provide the situational and contextual expertise that AI can't replicate. Many coaches now use PitchIQ alongside their sessions for exactly this reason.
