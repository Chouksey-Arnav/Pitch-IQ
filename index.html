📋 Before You Analyze
1
Pick the right moment
For batting: pause at bat-ball contact. For bowling: pause just as your front foot lands. These frames show the most meaningful biomechanical positions.
2
Check your angle
Side-on camera angle gives the most accurate readings. Front-on or back-on video will still work, but side-on is ideal — same view coaches use.
3
Use the frame buttons
Use ← → arrow keys or the Frame buttons below the video to step one frame at a time. Find the exact moment you want analyzed.
4
Your privacy is protected
Your video never leaves your device. All AI processing happens entirely in your browser — nothing is uploaded to any server.
Under the Hood

How PitchIQ Works

No special equipment. No internet required after loading. Just your video, your browser, and Google's AI pose estimation running locally.

01
🎬
Film yourself playing
Record yourself batting or bowling on any smartphone. Ask a teammate to hold the phone at waist height, 5–10 metres away, on the off side. No tripod or special setup needed — just a steady hand and good lighting.
02
🧠
AI maps 33 body points
Google MediaPipe Pose — the same technology used in professional sports analysis — runs entirely inside your browser. It detects every joint in your body and calculates precise angles at the exact frame you select. No upload. No account. Fully private.
03
📊
Get real coaching feedback
PitchIQ's cricket-specific feedback engine translates raw joint angles into coaching language — the same cues a qualified Level 3 coach would use. Elbow position, stance width, shoulder alignment, release height. You get a downloadable report you can take to your next training session.
